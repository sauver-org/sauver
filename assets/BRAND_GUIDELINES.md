## 1. The Core Strategy

Sauver is a digital sentry, an intelligent gatekeeper, and the initial resistance against automated inbox triage. We are moving from a developer's local utility toward an enterprise-ready inbox defense platform. Our visual identity must balance the raw energy of an open-source movement with the reliability, security, and precision expected from a professional security service.

### 2. The Sauver Shield (The Icon)

The primary graphical asset is the **Sauver Shield**. A shield is a direct metaphor for defense, a concept core to our function: a secure AI triage layer.

#### 2.1 Formal Description

The shield is a classical, pointed heater design (standard armor silhouette) partitioned **quarterly** (quartered) in a checkerboard pattern. This heraldic pattern signifies vigilance and historical protection, making our function intuitive.

#### 2.2 Correct Color Variant (The Standard)

This is the required configuration for all primary branding (website header, primary presentations). The quartering pattern must always be arranged as follows against our defined dark background:

- **Top-Left (Dexter Chief) and Bottom-Right (Sinister Base) Quarters:** Background Color.
- **Top-Right (Sinister Chief) and Bottom-Left (Dexter Base) Quarters:** Sauver Yellow.

_Do not rotate or mirror this quartering pattern._

## 3. Brand Colors

Our palette is limited and aggressive. It utilizes the high-visibility, hazard-prevention language common in security and critical operations.

### Primary Color: Sauver Yellow

This yellow is defined for authoritative visibility. It must not appear "muddy" (too brown) or "pastel" (too washed out). It is an optimized safety-yellow.

| Color Space      | Value                                                            |
| ---------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Hex (Web)**    | `#FAA116`                                                        |
| **RGB**          | `(250, 161, 22)`                                                 |
| **CMYK (Print)** | `(0, 42, 100, 2)` (Approximation—Use SVG for professional print) |
| **Pantone**      | **116 C**                                                        |

### Background & Secondary: Deep Charcoal

We define our dark background. _Avoid using `#000000` pure black for primary backgrounds,_ as it is visually harsh. This deep, rich charcoal provides a more sophisticated and less flat appearance, appropriate for an enterprise offering.

| Color Space   | Value          |
| ------------- | -------------- |
| **Hex (Web)** | `#111117`      |
| **RGB**       | `(17, 17, 23)` |

## 4. Typography

### Primary Font: **Sauver Caps**

The typography for the word "SAUVER" must convey authority, boldness, and unapologetic presence. It is a heavy, bold, condensed, uppercase sans-serif.

- **Reference Font:** Helvetica Neue Bold Condensed (Uppercase) is the standard reference.
- **Rules:**
  - **Always Uppercase:** "SAUVER" must never appear in lowercase in the logo.
  - **Letter Spacing (Tracking):** Maintain a subtle positive letter spacing (`0.1em` or `letter-spacing: 2px` in CSS) to prevent the condensed letters from looking crowded at small sizes.

## 5. Logo Variants & Usage

### 5.1 The Master Logo (Header)

This is the primary presentation on websites and professional multimedia. The **Shield Icon** is centered vertically with and precedes the word **SAUVER**.

- **Format:** SVG. (Use the `.svg` code provided in the previous turn).
- **Minimum Clearspace:** A mandatory "safe zone" must surround the Master Logo. No other text, icons, or complex graphical elements are permitted within this zone. The clearspace is equal to the full height of the text (H) on all sides.

### 5.2 Icon Only (The Sentinel)

The standalone Shield Icon may be used when the brand name "Sauver" is implied by context or available elsewhere on the page.

### 5.3 The Monotone Sentinel (Favicon & Overlays)

At small scales (e.g., a 16x16 browser favicon) or for simple overlays, the quartered pattern may lose definition and appear cluttered. In these specific cases, a monotone version of the shield (where the entire shield silhouette is solid Sauver Yellow) is permitted.

### 5.4 Vertical Logo (Stack)

For vertical contexts where space is limited (e.g., mobile footers, tall banners), the Shield Icon may be stacked centered above the text "SAUVER".

## 6. Prohibited Practices

We must protect the integrity of the Sentinel. The following usages are strictly prohibited:

- **Do not mirror or rotate the shield.**
- **Do not alter the quartering pattern.**
- **Do not apply gradients or complex textures.** Keep the design flat and bold.
- **Do not use `#000000` pure black backgrounds** for primary presentations.
- **Do not use the logo as a background pattern** unless reduced to `<5%` opacity.
- **Do not combine the Master Logo with other icons** (e.g., an email icon inside the shield) without formal approval.
